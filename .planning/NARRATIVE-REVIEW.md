## 1. Target Audience Fit — PASS (with one FLAG)

**Verdict:** PASS overall. Language is direct, Brazilian, operational. No academic fluff. CEO-level hooks ("resolve o mesmo problema todo dia", "Gestão no Excel e no WhatsApp", "Cresce contratando, nunca escalando") land correctly for a dono-de-empresa R$2M–R$50M.

**FLAG:** The slide 6 quote from Dario Amodei is intellectually heavy ("vantagem estrutural que levará décadas para ser igualada"). A CEO de médio porte brasileiro processes via resultado, not via tech-exec prophecy. The framing "O mesmo Claude já implementado em mais de 3.000 empresas brasileiras" directly below helps ground it, but the quote itself risks reading as pitch material for a VC, not a business owner. Suggest replacing with a Brazilian entrepreneur testimonial or removing the slide entirely and strengthening slide 11 (Resultados) instead.

## 4. ROI Narrative — FLAG

**Verdict:** FLAG. The investment slide (slide 12) shows:

> "Redução média de 3–8% em custo operacional. Em uma empresa de R$5M/ano = R$150k–R$400k de economia no 1° ano."

Two problems:

**FLAG 4a — The math doesn't land cleanly for the CEO.** R$100k investment vs R$150k–R$400k saving requires the CEO to trust the 3–8% assumption. That range is too wide (8% is nearly 3x the low end). For a CEO audience this creates doubt rather than conviction. A tighter claim tied to a specific sector example would be more credible. Example: "Uma transportadora com R$5M/ano que corta 60% das ligações de rastreamento economiza ~R$180k em custo de atendimento no primeiro ano."

**FLAG 4b — The dark version (propostadark) has stronger copy:** "O investimento se paga antes de você terminar de pagar." This is the best line in the entire deck. It's absent from the white version (propostawhite). The white version uses the softer "O investimento se paga. O lucro fica." — weaker. Sync the dark version's copy into the white deck.

**FLAG 4c — No concrete reference for the 3–8% range.** It reads as invented. If this comes from a McKinsey or Gartner benchmark, cite it. If not, remove the range and replace with a sector-specific example.

## 5. Niche Coverage — FLAG

**Verdict:** FLAG. The six niches in the deck (Contabilidade, Transportadora, Construtora, Varejo/Atacado, Franquia, Indústria/Distribuidora) are directionally correct for Brazilian R$2M+ mid-market companies.

**Missing high-value niches that the LP footer reveals EI already serves:**
- **Clínicas** — high-ticket, high pain around scheduling, prontuário, receita recorrente. Appears in the footer nav of both sector LPs.
- **Advocacia** — high-complexity, high fee per client. Also in footer nav.
- **Imobiliárias** — adjacent to Construtora already in deck, but distinct pain (lead qualification, CRM for corretor).
- **E-commerce** — appears in footer nav. High operational complexity, inventory + atendimento pain.

The deck shows 6 niches but the company already sells to at least 10. This creates a risk: a CEO from clínicas or advocacia sees the deck and doesn't recognize their business. If the deck is used as a general pitch (not sector-specific), add at least one reference to "e mais 6 setores" or expand the grid to 9 cards (3×3).

## 6. Copy Quality — FLAG (two instances)

**Verdict:** Two FLAGs. Overall Portuguese is Brazilian and direct. No americanisms detected in the main copy.

**FLAG 6a — "Done-for-you" in slide 7 (white version):** The word "Done-for-you" appears in English in the subtitle of slide 7 of propostawhite. Brazilian CEOs understand this term, but in a formal pitch to a company owner (not a marketer), it reads as SaaS-world jargon. Suggest replacing with "Feito por nós, implementado na sua empresa" or just removing the English — the slides below already communicate the concept better in Portuguese.

**FLAG 6b — "IA-First" throughout both decks:** The term appears 6+ times. It is intelligible but sounds like internal jargon that the target CEO (e.g., dono de transportadora, sócio de construtora) has never used. Suggest limiting to 1–2 uses maximum and replacing elsewhere with "empresa que opera com IA" or "empresa automatizada."

## 7. Statistics Accuracy — FAIL

**Verdict:** FAIL on three statistics. All three main stats (slide 2) are cited as research sources but the citations are not verifiable as stated:

| Stat | Cited source | Problem |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **73%** "das empresas brasileiras ainda tomam decisões sem dados em tempo real" | FGV/IBRE 2024 | No FGV/IBRE 2024 report with this specific finding is publicly verifiable. FGV IBRE produces macroeconomic indices, not this type of enterprise BI survey. This citation appears fabricated or misattributed. |
| **4×** "mais crescimento em empresas que adotaram IA em suas operações" | McKinsey 2024 | McKinsey publishes AI growth multipliers but "4×" as a standalone growth claim requires the specific report name (e.g., "The State of AI 2024"). Without the specific report title, this cannot be audited. A CEO or CFO who googles this before signing will not find it. |
| **67%** "redução de custo operacional em empresas que operaram com IA-first" | Gartner 2024 | Gartner does not publish a "67% cost reduction" benchmark for AI-first companies. Gartner research is behind paywall and sector-specific. This figure cannot be verified and appears fabricated or extrapolated incorrectly. |

**What to do:**
- Replace FGV/IBRE 2024 with a verifiable source or remove. Potential alternatives: TOTVS/Sebrae digital maturity reports, CNI Indústria 4.0 surveys, or Accenture Brazil AI report.
- For McKinsey 4×: cite "McKinsey Global Survey on AI, 2024" and add the specific finding about revenue growth — if the exact stat exists in McKinsey's published work. If it doesn't, use a stat that does (e.g., McKinsey 2024 reports that AI adopters see 10–15% revenue increase).
- For Gartner 67%: remove or replace with a verifiable Gartner Magic Quadrant or Hype Cycle finding. The slide 11 disclaimer "Baseado em dados de empresas que implementaram IA em operações — McKinsey, Gartner, FGV 2024" is also problematic for the same reason — the -67% figure is presented as an EI result but sourced to research about other companies' results, which is conflation.

This is the highest-risk item. A sophisticated CFO or CEO who fact-checks before R$100k will find these citations don't hold up.
